Skin Disease Classification and Malaria Specie classification from Medical Images

Over 30% of Nigerians live in rural areas with limited access to dermatologists and laboratory-based malaria testing. With fewer than 100 dermatologists serving over 200 million people and Nigeria accounting for 27% of global malaria deaths, there is an urgent need for low-cost, accessible diagnostic tools. Inspired by mobile microscopy and telemedicine, we developed an offline AI assistant that transforms any Android phone into a clinical decision support tool for frontline health workers.

The app integrates two key modules: a skin lesion classifier that identifies seven types of pigmented lesions and supports triage decisions, and a malaria parasite classifier that detects and distinguishes four Plasmodium species from blood smears to inform treatment. Built in Kotlin with TensorFlow Lite, the app runs fully offline, features a simple, icon-based interface with local language support, and is optimized for use in low-resource settings where internet access and electricity are limited.
